Types of Radiology

Main forms of Radiology

X-ray

The most common scan in NZ. Uses low low-dose radiation to image bones, lungs, and dense tissue. Quick and avaliable in every hospital.

Common uses: Broken bones, Chest infections, Dental imaging
Scan time: 5-15min
Radiation: Low

MRI

Uses magnets — no radiation. Produces the most detailed images of soft tissue, brain, spine, and joints. Essential in modern medicine.

Common uses: Brain & spine, Ligament injuries, Heart conditions
Scan time: 30-90min
Radiation: None

Ultrasound

Uses sound waves — completely safe, no radiation. Most commonly used for pregnancy scans and abdominal imaging.

Common uses: Pregnancy, Abdominal organs, Vascular checks
Scan time: 20-45min
Radiation: None

CT-scan

Combines hundreds of X-ray images into a 3D picture of the body. Used in emergency departments and for cancer diagnosis.

Common uses: Trauma & injuries, Cancer staging, Brain imaging
Scan time: 15-30min
Radiation: Moderate
